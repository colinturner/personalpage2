What I Intend to Build

Community Skills

A web app that pairs people who want to learn a new skill with mentors who offer their time for free - because they want to help out.

A prospective teacher describes their skills and availability. Students then submit applications to work alongside them. The website is meant to connect experience with hunger for knowledge.

e.g. Why not connect a retired carpenter with someone yearning to learn how to build? Our communities are wonderful resources - let's make use of them.

Community Bonds

An app that connects strangers for spontaneous friendly coffee breaks. I plan to partner with local caf√©s around London to offer users discounted prices when they treat each other to a cashless coffee through the app.

Imagine opening your phone and seeing a map full of dots denoting nearby app users. Your map tends to display those people who recently treated someone else to a coffee, in addition to app friends-of-friends who happen to be nearby. Click on their name and you've invited them to a free coffee session two minutes away!

Of course, personal profiles and messaging capabilities are built into the app. Within a few weeks you find yourself with a parallel, real-life social network thanks to the bonding power of the coffee bean. The point of this app is not necessarily dating, but to bring together people in a community who might otherwise never intersect.

Community Transportation

North America has high transportation costs and virtually no ride-sharing apps for inter-city travel. It's a huge

untapped field of opportunity.

My app will soon allow people to advertise unused seats in their car when they travel long distances. The free market will determine seat prices, but I expect them to be significantly lower than buses or trains, as is the case with similar apps on the European market.

I cannot wait to offer North Americans a cheaper, more environmentally-friendly and convivial way to travel with others.
How I Intend To Get There

Phase 1. Solo

Every journey of discovery begins alone and quickly snowballs into a group venture. My coding journey started almost three months ago in Montreal with a solo decision to improve my programming skills from zero to where they are now.

Fast-forward and I've spent the last two months in London learning to code with a wonderful community of like-minded people. It feels amazing to type this HTML right now!

Phase 2. Founders & Coders

My next phase is joining F&C full-time with the February cohort. I've attended virtually every meetup of the last two months and know and love the open, industrious, cross-disciplinary, community-driven (but self-directed) atmosphere. I want to keep giving and learning here in London.

My favourite moment thus far has been teaching 25 other F&C community members how to use Git and GitHub. It was an immensely rewarding opportunity to give back to a community that has already given so much to me - I certainly welcome the chance to give more tutorials to the community in 2017!

Phase 3. Start-ups

I plan to apply to London-based tech start-ups and mid-sized companies while attending F&C in Q1 2017. I want the opportunity to be a big fish in a small pond: to have lots of responsibility, but access to experienced coworkers to learn and grow from.

While working in the start-up scene, I want to remain engaged with the rest of the tech community, principally through F&C, to continue developing my own app ideas - a sampling of which are listed in the previous section.
